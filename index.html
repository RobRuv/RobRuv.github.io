Roberto Ruvalcaba

Education Major

I'm a first-generation college student at the University of California, Riverside, pursuing a B.A. in Education and Human Development. Growing up in Corona, California, I developed a strong interest in how educational systems impact students from diverse backgrounds. My academic journey has been shaped by a commitment to equity, inclusion, and student advocacy, values that continue to guide both my studies and my personal goals.

Throughout college, I've combined coursework with hands-on experience. My classes have focused on areas such as educational psychology, special education, instructional strategies, and policy analysis. I also volunteered at my local public library, where I supported youth programs, helped organize resources, and engaged with community members. This experience deepened my interest in working with children and families, particularly in educational settings that serve diverse populations.

Looking ahead, I plan to pursue a career in education and school psychology, with a focus on improving outcomes for underserved students. I'm currently building skills in behavioral intervention, IEP development, and data-driven support systems with the goal of helping schools become more inclusive and responsive. Whether in classrooms or policy spaces, I am driven by the belief that every student deserves the opportunity to thrive.
